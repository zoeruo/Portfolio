Deepdive

A learning and tracking App for freedivers

Overview

DeepDive is a unique app is designed to introduce users to the sport of freediving, enabling them to log and monitor their dives in a single, user-friendly platform. Notably, the app stands out by offering freediving lessons and providing divers with an all-in-one tool to track their progress. The app's sleek design and intuitive interface make it a refreshing and straightforward tool for all freediving enthusiasts.

Problem Statement

Diving is a thrilling and widely enjoyed sport, but it also poses risks when not executed with adequate training and safety measures. Although there are various resources available to help divers learn and keep track of their progress, such as instructor-led training, diving logs, and certification courses, there is a demand for a comprehensive mobile application that can assist divers in tracking their progress, learning new skills, and maintaining their safety.

Information Architecture

The “Deepdive” app focuses on helping users keep track of their learning progress. Besides providing an overview of the freediving stats, we include the journey into our scope.

Under “Learn,” we have “Lessons” and “Practice.” For the freediving sport, many on-land practices can help divers improve their freediving skills(e.g., breathing exercises, stretching, cardiovascular training, strength training, and mental preparation). These on-land practices will be included in the “Practice” session. In contrast, the other in-water techniques(e.g., equalization techniques, safety tips, respect for the environment, relaxation techniques, equipments, and diving techniques) will be included in the “Lessons” session.

Future Steps

  • Apple Watch Interface: Besides the mobile app, we plan to explore the interface for the Apple Watch tracking app. We’ll maintain the same visual styles as the mobile app. But since the Apple Watch has a small screen, we’ll design toward an intuitive, easy-to-use, and visually appealing UI with larger buttons and easy-to-read text.
  • Choosing appropriate data sensors: Understand the sensors needed for freediving data and choose reliable and accurate sensors that can be accessed through the Apple Watch.
  • Allowing learners to subscribe to a premium version that provides personalized learning and tracking
  • Usability Test: Testing the prototype with a group of users to identify usability issues and make further adjustments.
  • Incorporate safety features: Since freediving is an extreme sport, safety is paramount in freediving. Any careless can lead to serious issues. Therefore, we will explore the possible safety features for our app.(e.g., depth alarm, emergency contact information)

Takeaways

  • Simply the UI: The interface should be straightforward and easy to use, ensuring that users can access information about diving journey, dive logs, and lessons quickly.
  • Considering more complex and interesting tasks: We may find greater opportunities for improvement by considering and exploring additional possibilities. The inspiration can come from the competitive analysis we performed.
  • Consider safety features: Safety should always be among the top priorities when it comes to designing a freediving app. We should reconsider the possibilities to integrate the features into the app in the future.
  • Simplified but focused scenario: The scenario should be focused on a specific user’s motivations, goals, challenges, and actions. We don’t need to include many steps to create a helpful user scenario.
  • Interesting and focused persona: We learned how to make persona interesting but also include necessary information that can help with design such as pain-points, needs, behavioral traits, goals and context of use.
