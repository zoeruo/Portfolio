Cheng Hsin General Hospital

Responsive Web Design

Overview

Cheng Hsin General Hospital is a modern general hospital located in Taipei, and it is famous for cardiology. The goal of this project was to build a responsive website featuring the new international medical services and provide more convenient online services for the general public

Role

Date

Background

The previous hospital website was built almost ten years ago. Since the hospital's services had expanded, the old website could no longer support the vast information. For example, the newly established International Medical Services Center required a multi-language website for international patients. Moreover, it was highly inconvenient to departments that they couldn't edit their information anytime on the old website because it was outsourced. The project includes four websites: The Official Website, a Multi-Language Website, Departments Websites, and a Content Management System.

I was the sole designer alongside one system analyst and three web developers in the initial stage of the project. I was the only one left completing the remaining codes and maintaining the website in the late stage. My key achievements are listed as below:

  • Integrated the needs of various departments.
  • Executed from design to web development.
  • Improved usability according to feedbacks from users solely.

Redesign Process  /  Information Architecture

The Official Website

After understanding the old website's structure, I added components and categorized the information. For instance, people can find common services under the Health Information section.  In addition, I canceled the subscription function since no more than a hundred people were still subscribing. Instead, I added a section for the general public to download the newsletters.

Multi-Language Website

The international website was designed to provide simpler but useful and clear information. As for the Leading Specialties, editors have two options for adding content. One is to select articles from the departments' websites, which departments' members edited, and insert them into the section; the other is to insert brand new articles. 

Departments Websites

About is the homepage of the website. The eight sections below are the functions the website provides. Every department can choose to add or remove the sections. For example, editors can remove the Events link from the menu if a department doesn't want to include event information or doesn't even hold events.

Content Management Website

The website includes content management and authority management function. By defining each role's access to the website’s contents and assigning roles to accounts, employees in the hospital can log in to the system and edit the parts they are responsible for.

Redesign Process  /  Low-fidelity Prototyping

With the clock ticking, I quickly drew the flows and confirmed with the system analyst that the database design would support the functions I wanted to provide to the users.

Redesign Process  /  High-fidelity Prototyping

High-fidelity Prototyping allowed me to discuss the details and styling of content on different screen sizes with the deputy superintendent. Based on the hospital's logo, which contains an orchid with a tenet meant to serve the public with charity, I opted for a warm look to convey the aim to the general public.

Redesign Process  /  Designs

Multi-Language Website

Users can switch languages from the upper right corner on every page. The editors can edit the specific language page instead of inserting information for all three languages. When a user switches the language on a page with no corresponding language, the user will be redirected to the previous page.

The Official Website  /  Newsletter

The newest volume is displayed at the top. When a mouse hovers over a volume, it will flip to show its title and summary. People can download them simply with a click.

404 Not Found

To create a good experience for users, I customized a 404 page to inform them that the article is gone.

Development

Our team used .NET to build the four websites. As soon as I confirmed the design, I started to develop the websites with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Other engineers started from the back-end codes. The schedule was intense. Due to the time limit, we were developing and testing simultaneously. When the project was about 80% to completion, my teammates withdrew from the project to work on other systems. I finished the websites and published them on my own. In the development process, I received feedback directly and improved the functions at every stage by collecting their demands.

Results

Since implementing the new responsive website, we have seen a significant increase in online appointments and international patients. In addition, I have received positive feedback from the users about the concise and straightforward functions, allowing them to show people their features and saving them a large proportion of their time.
